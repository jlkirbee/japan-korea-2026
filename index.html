üéå Etiquette & Phrases

üáØüáµ Japan

  • No tipping. Ever. It's considered rude.
  • Shoes off indoors ‚Äî ryokans, some restaurants, temples.
  • Chopstick rules: Don't stick upright in rice (funeral). Don't pass food chopstick-to-chopstick.
  • Quiet on trains. No phone calls, whisper conversations.
  • Line up. Queuing culture is sacred. Don't cut.
  • Bow slightly when greeting or thanking.
  • "Sumimasen" ‚Äî your most useful word. Excuse me / sorry / thank you.
  • Trash cans are rare. Carry your trash. Konbini have bins inside.

üá∞üá∑ Korea

  • No tipping. Same as Japan.
  • Shoes off at traditional restaurants and homes.
  • Pour drinks for others (especially elders) with two hands. Turn away from elders when drinking.
  • Age matters. If asked your age, it's cultural curiosity.
  • "Kamsahamnida" = thank you. Most useful Korean phrase.
  • "Bballi bballi" = hurry hurry. Korea runs fast. Don't dawdle on escalators.
